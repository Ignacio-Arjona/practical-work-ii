Degree (1st Year)

This section will provide an in-depth look at your degree, covering its purpose, structure, and a list of subjects, with a dedicated page on "Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" exploring its content, objectives, and learning outcomes.

Overview

Purpose

Structure

ALGEBRA I

The main objective of the Algebra I course is to introduce students to the world of abstract mathematics through the understanding and application of advanced techniques in real-life problems. Students will develop reasoning and logical deduction skills through the use of algebra as the appropriate tool for problem-solving.

CALCULUS I

The main objective of the course is to develop the ability to use differential and integral calculus of real functions of a single variable. This is done in a way that the student understands its fundamentals and is capable of applying it to problems of optimization, rate of change, finding extrema, approximations, graphical representation of functions, and, finally, the calculation of areas and volumes.

PROGRAMMING I

The final objective of the course is to introduce students to the principles of systematic problem-solving through the design of algorithms and coding a solution, as well as the basic rules of computation.

LOGIC AND CRITICAL THINKING

The course Logic and Critical Thinking provides the necessary foundation and key skills for the current development and training as a Mathematical Engineer, as well as for the subsequent development of research, analysis, and action capabilities in the field of Mathematical Engineering applications.

FUNDAMENTALS OF COMPUTER ENGINEERING

The specific objectives that this course aims to cover are:
-Understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, the ultimate goal it pursues, and the benefit it brings to society, reflecting on the role each person wants to play as an engineer in the society of the future.
-Learn about the pillars on which technological progress must be based, gaining an initial historical perspective of technological development.

ALGEBRA II

The final objective of this course is for the student to gain a deep understanding and mathematical proficiency in topics related to algebra, such as matrix diagonalization, obtaining the Jordan form, affine and Euclidean geometry, as well as the elementary theory of groups and rings.

CALCULUS II

The main objective of the course is to train professionals who develop effective thinking, with analytical and diagnostic skills to make decisions, argue, and engage in other creative or critical actions, while being consistent and honest in their judgments.

DISCRETE MATHEMATICS

The main objectives of the Discrete Mathematics course are to apply set theory and graph theory, handle finite and modular arithmetic, use logical and recursive methods to formalize reasoning in a systematic way, and prove the existence of properties of infinite sets through mathematical induction. Finally, the course aims to synthesize, in a finite number of rules and in a recursive manner, the generation of an infinite number of elements.

MATHEMATICAL AND COMPUTATIONAL LOGIC

Its objective is to understand and analyze valid and correct reasoning. Logic deals with the rules and principles that govern thought and reasoning, providing tools to evaluate the validity of arguments and the coherence of thought.

HISTORY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY

The aim is for the student to gain a broad overview of the most significant events that have framed the advances in science and technology.

INTEGRATIVE PROJECT I

By the end of this course, the student should have acquired the necessary skills to solve complex problems that involve prior research to find the most suitable solution, resolve the problem, and professionally present the results.

HISTORY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY

The objective of the course is for the student to deepen their self-knowledge; understand the habits they need to develop to foster personal maturity; discover the meaning of the university stage in their life and the value of university culture as a communal search for knowledge; develop sensitivity towards the university methodology, and grow in all areas in a comprehensive manner.

ELECTRONICS AND COMPUTER TECHNOLOGY

The main objective is, on one hand, to learn the fundamentals of electronics and its relation to the physical operation of computers, peripherals, and communication systems used in computer science, and on the other hand, to understand the principles of logical design and apply techniques for designing simple digital circuits.
