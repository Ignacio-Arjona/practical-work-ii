Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

Content


The subject "Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" is structured into two main parts to provide both theoretical knowledge and practical skills essential for computer engineering:

Theoretical Foundations


Block 1 (Units I-III):
Background and historical perspectives (I), The current context of Computer Science (II), Computer Engineering as a Degree and Profession (III)

Explores the historical evolution of Information Technology, including past, present, and future perspectives. Discusses the role of computer engineering as an academic discipline and profession, with a view on ethical principles and the human element in technology.

Block 2 (Units IV-VIII):
Hardware (IV), Software (V), Operating Systems (VI), Networks (VII), New Trends (VIII)

Examines key concepts in software development, the structure and functionality of hardware, software, and networks, as well as emerging trends such as cloud computing, quantum computing, cognitive computing, blockchain, and IoT (Internet of Things).

Block 3 (Units IX-X):
Information and Data (IX), Information and Big Data (X)

Focuses on the representation of digital information, organization and modeling of data, challenges in Big Data, and data protection strategies.

Practical Skills



Block 1:
-Introduction to fundamental concepts of software development and web design

Block 2:
-Introduction to fundamental concepts of HTML5 information management with CSS3

Block 3:
-Introduction to fundamental concepts of design and layout with CSS3, including selectors, box modeling and element positioning

Objectives


The course is designed to achieve the following objectives:

Objectives


-Understand the general purpose of IT, its goals, and its contribution to society. Encourage students to reflect on their role as future engineers.
-Explore the historical and foundational pillars of technology, contributing to gain a broader understanding of the evolution of the field.
-Highlight the importance of ethical practices and the human factor in the engineering profession, while implanting a sense of responsibility.
-Provide an overview of the different areas of computer engineering, enabling students to distinguish potential career paths and specializations.
-Introduce students to key concepts in modern computing paradigms, data management, and foundational web development.

Learning Outcomes


By the end of the course, students will:

Outcomes


-Be capable of identifying and applying technical terminology related to hardware, software, and systems in the context of computer engineering.
-Relate and articulate key historical events that shaped the field of computing and analyze future technological trends.
-Understand and apply methods for the representation, organization, and management of digital information.
-Explain foundational concepts in the three primary areas of computer engineering: hardware, software, and networks.
-Design and implement a web-based system for information dissemination, demonstrating knowledge of web technologies.
-Investigate and present IT-related projects, showcasing both written and oral communication skills for technical and non-technical audiences.
